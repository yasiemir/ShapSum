State-run media indicated that a crackdown had begun  against the people of Armenia who continue to agitate for annexation  of a mostly Armenian enclave in the neighboring republic of  Azerbaijan.
Mikail Gorbachev proposed tougher legislation to punish Soviets who  kindle ethnic unrest, saying "nationalist passions" benefit opponents  of his reforms.
Gorbachev said  the grounds for tougher legislation come from the Soviet constitution,  which says it is unlawful to advocate "racial or national  exclusiveness, hostility or contempt".
Mikail Gorbachev warned the nation that the country's growing ethnic  strife is endangering its unity as a state as well as threatening his  program of political and economic reform.
The six-month conflict between the two republics over the  Nagorno- Karabakh district has let to ethnic violence.
Nearly 300 people  have been killed in clashes in the past two years.
The conflict, stems directly  from the breakup of the Soviet Union, unlike the more serious strife  in Nagorno-Karabakh where tension between Azeris and Armenians has  existed for centuries.
Troops of the former Soviet Union were ordered out of the Armenian  enclave of Nagorno-Karabakh after Armenian and Azeri militants fired  on them.
