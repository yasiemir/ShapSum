Thunderstorms with tornadoes, 100-mph winds and hail wreaked havoc across the middle   Mississippi Valley and the Southeast Wednesday.
The   thunderstorms developed ahead of a strong cold front and swept eastward from Missouri and   Arkansas.
Elsewhere, snow fell from the northern   Rockies to the upper Great Lakes, while unseasonably mild weather continued in the East.
Four tornadoes pounded the Huntsville, AL area killing two people and causing injuries.
Southern Illinois was hard hit by storms with winds 80-100   mph.
Thunderstorms dropped large hailstones in Indiana and Tennessee.
65 mph winds damaged   Kentucky and Louisiana.
Another   touched down in South Carolina.
Blocked roads made it difficult for doctors to reach the Humana Hospital.
Strong winds and heavy thunderstorms caused heavy damage in the District of Columbia,   Illinois, Maryland, Delaware, New Jersey, New York, Pennsylvania and West Virginia.
Two other tornadoes struck as severe weather swept across much of Alabama.
A tornado slammed into a shopping district, a residential area, and a school Wednesday in   Huntsville, AL, killing at least 14 people, injuring nearly 300 and trapping many others in cars   and homes.
