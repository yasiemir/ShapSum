Late this afternoon   the water level at the Changsha monitoring station had risen to 38.93 meters, some 0.56   meters higher than ever previously recorded.
So far, more than 500,000 soldiers and local residents are fighting the floods.
Yesterday afternoon 19 dykes in the Changsha area collapsed affecting 128,000 residents.
Floods have hit Changsha, the capital of central China's Hunan Province following a   succession of rainstorms in the upper and middle Xiangjiang River.
The water level is still rising sharply.
Jiang Zemin, general secretary of the CPC Central Committee Political Bureau and   chairman of the Central Military Commission, and Li Peng, premier of the State Council,   are very concerned about floods in Guangdong Province.
The Changsha city government has asked those in the endangered area to move to safer   places.
Communist Party officials urge perseverance.
They gave important directives   for current flood prevention and disaster relief tasks in Guangdong.
Floods continue to seriously affect Guangdong, Hunan and Jiangxi Provinces and the   Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region.
At Wuzhou, a city on the Xunjiang River, the   floodwater's speed was recorded at 46,200 cubic-meters-per-second, the most ferocious   since the founding of New China in 1949.
