An operation to lower the cholesterol levels of heart attack patients reduced their risk of   another heart attack or death from heart disease.
Doctor's also found that one aspirin and a single dose of clot   dissolving given within 24 hours of heart attack dramatically cut the risk of a second fatal   heart attack.
Since 60% of   heart attacks occur before the victim reaches the hospital, a major goal is getting people   to go to the hospital at the first sign of a heart attack.
A drug that eases the heart's workload can cut the risk of new cardiac attacks and death   by 20 percent in people recovering from heart attacks.
The drug, Verapanil, a calcium   channel blocker is about as effective as another variety of blood pressure medicines   known as beta blockers at protecting people after heart attacks.
The TGF beta seems to block the action of other substances, such as   tumor necrosis factor, that can cause blood vessels to narrow following a heart attack.
An experiment with more than 1700 high blood pressure patients showed that a blood and   urine test can pinpoint those patients most likely to have a heart attack.
